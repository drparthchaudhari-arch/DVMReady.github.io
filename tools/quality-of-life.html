Quality of Life Calculator

HHHHHMM Scale for end-of-life decision making. Evaluate Hurt, Hunger, Hydration, Hygiene, Happiness, Mobility, and More good days than bad.

When to Use This Tool

This assessment helps evaluate whether your patient's quality of life is acceptable or if humane euthanasia should be considered. Score each category honestly. A total score of 35+ suggests acceptable quality of life. Below 35, consider palliative care or euthanasia.

üìã Interpretation Guide

35+ Points = Acceptable Quality of Life

Continue current care, reassess regularly. Monitor for changes.

25-34 Points = Marginal Quality of Life

Consider palliative care, hospice, or intensive intervention. Reassess frequently (weekly).

Below 25 Points = Poor Quality of Life

Humane euthanasia should be considered to prevent suffering. Discuss with family.

Reference: HHHHHMM Scale adapted from Villalobos, A. (2004). "Canine and Feline Geriatric Oncology: Honoring the Human-Animal Bond." This tool is a guide only. Always discuss end-of-life decisions with your veterinarian.
