Production Animal Case

Bovine Clinical Mastitis

URL: https://parthchaudhari.com/bridge/case-studies/bovine-mastitis.html

← Back to Study Games

Signalment and Presenting Complaint

  • BreedHolstein dairy cow
  • Age5 years
  • Lactation3rd lactation, 45 days postpartum
  • Presenting ComplaintReduced milk production with abnormal quarter secretion

History

Farm staff reported acute drop in output from a single quarter during morning milking, with visible flakes and clots in milk stripping. The cow was quieter than normal and separated from herd activity.

Recent wet bedding conditions and variable teat hygiene compliance were noted over the prior week. No recent calving complications were reported.

Physical Exam Findings

  • Left rear quarter enlarged, warm, and painful to palpation
  • Abnormal milk: watery secretion with flakes/clots
  • Rectal temperature 39.8°C
  • Reduced feed intake and mild depression
  • No neurologic deficits or recumbency

Problem List

  1. Quarter-specific inflammation with painful udder changes
  2. Abnormal milk consistency and appearance
  3. Mild pyrexia with reduced appetite
  4. Potential herd-level infectious pressure

Differentials (Ranked)

  1. Clinical mastitis (bacterial)
  2. Traumatic teat/udder injury with secondary inflammation
  3. Systemic illness with nonspecific milk drop

Treatment Overview

Management includes frequent stripping of the affected quarter, anti-inflammatory support, and targeted intramammary/systemic therapy based on likely severity and culture guidance. Milking hygiene reinforcement is essential to reduce recurrence.

Key Learning Points

  • Quarter-level signs and milk changes are high-yield indicators of clinical mastitis.
  • Sterile milk culture supports targeted and defensible treatment plans.
  • Herd management factors are often part of the causal chain.
  • Prevention workflows are as important as acute case treatment.
